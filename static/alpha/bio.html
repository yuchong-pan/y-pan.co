Yuchong Pan is a first-year student in the Faculty of Science at The University of British Columbia. He took a gap year before studying at UBC.

Yuchong graduated from Shaoxing No.1 High School. During high school, Yuchong participated in several algorithmic contests, including the NOI (National Olympiad in Informatics) series and online competitions. In 2015, he won a Silver Medal at IOI (International Olympiad in Information) China Team Selection Competition and a Bronze Medal at Asia Pacific Informatics Olympiad, and was placed in Top 500 at Round 2 of Google Code Jam.

In his gap year, Yuchong served as the first Gap Year Tech Intern at InitialView, the leading interview company in the world, during which he helped build the front-end and the back-end of InitialView's website and mobile apps. Yuchong previously worked at Sogou Inc., a leading Internet company in China that provides various Internet products and services including search engines, IME and web browsers. From his internship experiences, Yuchong gradually develops his skills of full-stack web development.

Yuchong is a hackathon enthusiast. He has participated in 5 hackathons, created several interesting projects and received some awards since October 2016. For instance, he once built Vox, a tool that redefines information structures for audio documents, and GitArt, an idea-sharing and collaboration platform for artists and story tellers based on Git. Here is a list of Yuchong's projects:

  • Vox, a tool that redefines information structures for audio documents with visualization and vertical search, i-Lab Hackathon (June 2017)
  • GitArt, an idea sharing and collaboration platform for artists and story tellers based on Git, Unique Hackday (June 2017)
  • Viso, a coding interview platform with Leap Motion anti-cheating system, Hack(Nanjing) (May 2017)
  • Aquatopia, a extensible monitoring, alerting and visualization system for environmental data, PennApps XV (January 2017)
  • Hack Search, a vertical, distributed search engine for programming green hands, HACKxFDU (October 2016)
  • e-word, a desktop app for English vocabulary memorizing built in Delphi (2012)

Yuchong met Audrey, Shaolin and Yiluo at the PennApps XV Hackathon at University of Pennsylvania, and co-founded the hack.init() Hackathon with them. The hack.init() Hackathon is one of the premier hackathons in China, and the first hack.init() Hackathon was held at ShanghaiTech University in Shanghai during July 7-8, 2017. Also, Yuchong serves as Director of Tech Department and an Executive Board Member at The hack.init() Team; he is the sole developer of the hackathon's website.
